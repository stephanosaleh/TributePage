Dame Zaha Mohammad Hadid

The "Queen of Curves", who "liberated architectural geometry, giving it a whole new expressive identity".

A brief timeline highlighting some of her key accomplishments:

    Early Career and Education

  • 1950: Born on October 31 in Baghdad, Iraq.
  • 1972: Graduated from the Architectural Association School of Architecture in London.
    Architectural Career Milestones

  • 1980s: Established her own practice in London.
  • 1993: Won the competition to design the Vitra Fire Station in Germany, her first major commission.
  • 2000: Completed the Bergisel Ski Jump in Innsbruck, Austria, gaining international recognition.
  • 2004: Became the first woman to win the Pritzker Architecture Prize, the highest honor in architecture.
  • 2010: Designed the London Aquatics Centre for the 2012 Olympics.
  • 2014: Completed the Heydar Aliyev Center in Baku, Azerbaijan, known for its fluid, futuristic design.
  • 2016: Passed away on March 31 in Miami, Florida, leaving a lasting legacy in architecture.
    Architectural Career Milestones

  • Known for her visionary designs characterized by sweeping curves, fluid forms, and innovative use of materials.
  • Pushed the boundaries of architecture, blending art, technology, and avant-garde aesthetics.
  • Continued to influence architectural discourse posthumously through her firm, Zaha Hadid Architects.


Zaha Hadid's career spanned decades and left an indelible mark on the field of architecture, inspiring future generations with her bold and visionary approach to design.
